Brand voice & tone — 3 defining adjectives

Warm

Speaks like a trusted family cook — knowledgeable but never preachy. Celebrates home traditions with genuine affection.

Honest

Says exactly what's in the jar. No marketing fluff. If it's fresh, say it. If it's spicy, warn them. Earns trust through transparency.

Rooted

Deeply grounded in regional Indian culinary heritage. Names recipes by origin. Respects the craft behind every chutney.

In practice: Write copy as if a knowledgeable farmer's daughter is narrating — confident about ingredients, proud of heritage, never corporate. Use "we" sparingly; let the product speak. Avoid: buzzwords like "artisanal", "curated", "crafted experience". Prefer: "stone-ground", "sun-dried", "made this week".

3 brand messaging pillars

Purity you can taste

No preservatives. No artificial colours. No shortcuts. Every jar contains ingredients you'd recognise from your grandmother's kitchen — and nothing else.

Proof point: shelf life 30 days, always refrigerated

Recipes with a regional soul

Each chutney traces back to a specific regional tradition — Maharashtrian Thecha, Coastal Coconut, Pune-style Garlic. Geography is flavour.

Proof point: origin story on every product page

Made this week, not months ago

Freshness is a verb, not a label. Farm Puro batches are made to order. You'll see the batch date on every jar — because we're proud of it.

Proof point: batch date printed on every lid

Emotional benefits — lead with these on the homepage

The taste of home, wherever home is

For NRIs in London or Bengaluru professionals who grew up on homemade chutneys — Farm Puro is the jar that closes the distance. Open it and smell your mother's kitchen.

Feed your family what you can actually trust

You read labels. You care about what your children eat. Farm Puro is for parents who refuse to compromise — premium price, zero compromise on ingredients.

Eat well without spending your Sunday cooking

Urban professionals deserve restaurant-quality accompaniments on a Tuesday night. Farm Puro turns a simple dal-roti into something special — in the time it takes to open a jar.
