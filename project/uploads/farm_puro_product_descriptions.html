Farm Puro Chutneys — all 9 product descriptions

01 · Sweet

Tamarind Date Chutney

"The one that makes everything taste like a celebration."

Sun-dried Tamarind

Thick, dark, and deeply sweet-tangy — the kind of chutney that transforms a plain samosa into a memory. Medjool dates round off the tamarind's pucker with a natural caramel softness. Spoon it over chaats, swirl it into dahi, or let it do its quiet magic as a dipping sauce for anything fried.

Best paired with

Samosa & kachori with masala chai
Dahi puri & sev puri chaat
Kebab platter as a table chutney

02 · Light

Coconut Chutney

"Cool, coastal, and ready in seconds."

Fresh-scraped Coconut

Pale, creamy, and tempered with a mustard-curry leaf tadka that pops the moment it hits the jar. This is South Indian breakfast in a spoon — mild enough for children, layered enough for those who know what they're tasting. Pairs with idli and dosa, but holds its own alongside any mildly spiced dish.

Best paired with

Idli-dosa breakfast spread
Pesarattu & uttapam
Spread on multigrain toast for a light lunch

03 · Bold

Garlic Red Chutney

"The loudest jar on the table — and the first empty."

Whole Kashmiri Chillies

Deep red, aggressively garlicky, with a slow-building heat that settles warmly in the chest. Stone-ground in small batches so the garlic stays sharp, not bitter. This is the chutney that converts people — one bite with a vada pav and nothing else quite satisfies again. Use it boldly; it won't apologise.

Best paired with

Vada pav & batata vada
Tandoori roti with dal makhani
Grilled paneer & chicken tikka marinade

04 · Cooling

Coriander Mint Chutney

"Bright, herby, and instantly refreshing."

Garden-fresh Coriander

The colour of early monsoon — vivid green with a clean herbal bite and a whisper of lime. Made from coriander and mint picked at peak freshness, ground same-day so nothing goes dull. Works as a cooling counterpoint to anything rich or spiced, and doubles as the world's best sandwich spread.

Best paired with

Seekh kebab & shami kebab platter
Dhokla & khandvi snack board
Spread inside kathi rolls & frankie

05 · Staple

Ginger-Garlic Paste

"The base of every great Indian dish, done right."

Fresh-pressed, no vinegar

No vinegar. No filler. Just raw ginger and whole garlic, ground together at the right ratio — 40:60, the way most home cooks have always known it tastes best. Pungent, honest, and ready to go straight into the kadhai without the sting of preservatives. Your cooking will taste the difference immediately.

Best paired with

Mutton & chicken curries as the base
Marinades for tikka & tandoori dishes
Rajma-chawal & dal tadka

06 · Everyday

Tomato Onion Chutney

"The chutney that goes with absolutely everything."

Vine-ripened Tomatoes

Slow-cooked until the tomatoes collapse and the onions turn sweet, with just enough chilli to remind you this is Indian cooking. Jammy, slightly smoky, and versatile in the truest sense — it belongs at breakfast alongside idli as much as it does at dinner next to phulkas. The jar that never gets old.

Best paired with

Idli-dosa & South Indian tiffin
Plain phulka with ghee
Egg bhurji & akuri

07 · Aromatic

Curry Leaf Chutney

"The underrated hero of South Indian cooking."

Fresh Curry Leaves, hand-picked

Dark olive-green, earthy, and deeply aromatic in the way only fresh curry leaves can be — a scent that fills a kitchen and a flavour you can't quite name but always recognise. Blended with roasted chana dal and green chilli for texture and heat. Rare to find outside home kitchens. Rarer to find made fresh.

Best paired with

Rava idli & set dosa
Mixed into curd rice for depth
Alongside Chettinad & Andhra curries

08 · Fiery

Green Chilli Thecha

"Maharashtrian fire, pounded not ground."

Hirvi Mirchi, stone-pounded

Coarse, chunky, fiercely hot — thecha is not a chutney, it's a statement. Pounded (never blended) with roasted peanuts and garlic until you have something that bites back. The texture is everything: uneven, rustic, alive. A spoonful alongside bhakri and thick curd is lunch that asks nothing else of you.

Best paired with

Jowar bhakri with raw onion & curd
Varan-bhat (Maharashtrian dal-rice)
Thalipeeth with white butter

09 · Dry

Peanut Garlic Chutney

"Dry, nutty, garlicky — and dangerously addictive."

Dry-roasted Vidarbha Peanuts

Coarsely ground, never smooth — the dry chutney that Maharashtrian households keep in a steel dabba and reach for daily. Roasted peanuts give it a toasty depth; raw garlic cuts through with a clean sharpness; red chilli brings warmth without heat. Sprinkle on poha, mix into curd, or eat directly off the spoon.

Best paired with

Poha & upma at breakfast
Sprinkled on sabudana khichdi
Bhakri with oil & alongside misal pav
