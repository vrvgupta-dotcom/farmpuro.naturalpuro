Our story

From a Pune kitchen to your dining table.

Farm Puro began in 1987 in Aaji's tiny third-floor flat in Sadashiv Peth, where she ground chutneys on a stone paata for half the neighbourhood. The neighbours brought back empty jars and ten-rupee notes. The recipes stayed the same.

Three generations later, we still cook in small copper kadhais, still source our tamarind from the same orchard in Andhra, and still refuse to add a single thing that doesn't belong. Every jar is a promise — that what tasted like home then, will taste like home now.

The Farm Puro promise

Why we make chutney differently

No shortcuts, no synthetics, no compromises. Just the way it was made before factories decided otherwise.

01

Farm-direct sourcing

We buy tamarind from Andhra orchards, coconuts from Kerala backyards and chillies from Guntur — paying farmers 20% above market price and skipping every middleman in between.

02

Hand-ground, small batch

Every jar starts on a stone slab, not a steel blade. We cook in 8-kilo copper kadhais — slow enough to coax out the flavour, small enough to taste-test by the spoonful.

03

Zero preservatives, FSSAI-certified

Cold-filled into amber glass, sealed within four hours of cooking and refrigerated through transit. The shelf life is shorter — the taste is the same as the day it was made.
